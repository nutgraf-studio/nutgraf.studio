Hero illustration & typography options

Sixteen minimalistic, animated alternatives for the index-page header, each shown inside a replica of the live hero (mint background, real headline) so they can be judged in context. Options 7–10 are a second pass: more abstract, with a 3D feel, all built on rotation. Options 11–13 are a third pass: remixes — the icosahedron crossed with the streams, a higher-energy icosahedron, and the streams pushed into 3D. Options 14–16 take the tumbling icosahedron further, surrounding it with floating elements: orbiting satellites, drifting strings, a linked subnetwork. Everything is inline SVG/CSS plus a little vanilla JS for the 3D projections — no libraries, no images — and all freeze gracefully under prefers-reduced-motion. The current cube network is included at the top for reference. A typography pass (T1–T7) proposes alternative typography for the whole site: each pairing is shown in the hero and in a specimen card with headings, body copy, a stat and UI elements. All faces are free Google Fonts, so any option is a straight swap of the two font variables in index.html. A final pass (E1–E3) animates the word stories in the headline itself with a violet accent: a marker underline, a highlighter sweep and a scribbled circle.

Fourth pass — typography

Seven pairings for the whole site, judged in the hero and in a specimen card (headings, body, stat, labels, button). The current pairing — Fraunces 900 for display, Inter for body — is what every option above uses; each proposal below replaces one or both voices.

T1

Space Grotesk + Inter — the technologist

Drops the serif entirely for a geometric grotesque with just enough quirk (the flat-topped lowercase, the angular joins). Reads product, dashboards, tools — the most "data" and least "editorial" direction. Body stays Inter, so it's a one-font swap. No italics exist, so the emphasised word takes a violet underline instead.

Fifth pass — animating the word "stories"

Three violet treatments applied directly to the emphasised word in the headline. Each one draws in, holds for a few seconds, fades and redraws — and each is shown against the current cube-network visual so the two animations can be judged together. Under prefers-reduced-motion the effect holds at its finished state.

E1

Marker underline

A hand-drawn violet stroke sweeps under the word left to right, like a marker pass, holds, lifts and redraws. The gentlest of the three — it decorates without touching the letterforms, so the headline stays perfectly readable throughout.

Highlighter sweep

A violet band wipes in behind the lower half of the word like a highlighter pass, sits for a few seconds, then retracts. Pure CSS on the <em> — no extra markup at all — and the boldest of the three: a solid block of brand colour inside the headline.

Scribble circle
