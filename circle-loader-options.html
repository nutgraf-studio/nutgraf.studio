Hero circle — purple loader options

Four ways the purple line can travel along the white circle behind the icosahedron. Each mock uses the real hero colors and geometry (mint background, white circle at r=245, violet #7B7BF0). Speed, line thickness, arc length and dot size are all easy to tune once you pick a direction.

AComet sweep

A quarter-circle arc orbits continuously at constant speed, dot leading the way. Never stops, never resets — calm and steady.

BTrace & reset

The line draws the full circle from the top with the dot at its tip, holds the completed ring for a beat, fades out and starts over. Reads most like "loading".

CDraw & unwind

The line fills the whole circle, then its tail sweeps around and catches up, erasing it — a continuous wind/unwind cycle with no fade or hard reset.

DComet with fading tail

Same steady orbit as A, but the line tapers and fades out behind the dot, like a shooting-star trail. The most "alive" of the four.

EThin arc sweep

A hairline quarter-circle arc orbits continuously at constant speed. No dot — the quietest, most minimal option.

FThin draw & unwind

A hairline stroke draws the full circle from the top, then its tail sweeps around and erases it — continuous wind/unwind, no dot, no fade.
